MYSTERIES OF GALAXY

Galaxies, vast collections of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter, hold numerous mysteries. One of the biggest is the nature of dark matter and dark energy, which together make up the vast majority of the universe's mass-energy content but remain undetected directly. The formation and evolution of galaxies, including the role of mergers and interactions, are also active areas of research. Furthermore, the existence of supermassive black holes at the centers of most galaxies, and how they influence galactic evolution, is another enduring puzzle. Finally, the discovery of galaxies with little or no dark matter challenges our understanding of galaxy formation and could provide clues about the nature of dark matter itself.
